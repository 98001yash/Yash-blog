Engineering blogs are often treated like marketing surfaces, but the best ones feel more like notebooks. They preserve the reasoning behind decisions, not just the polished outcome.

## Clarity is part of the work

When a team explains why something was built a certain way, future decisions become easier. A useful blog post does not only announce what shipped. It makes the tradeoffs legible.

## Good writing reduces technical friction

A thoughtful article can help in several ways:

- it captures the context around a decision
- it teaches patterns through concrete examples
- it gives future teammates a durable reference point

## Minimal interfaces help writing stand out

A writing-first site should stay quiet. Typography, spacing, and rhythm should carry the experience. When the interface gets out of the way, ideas have more room to breathe.
